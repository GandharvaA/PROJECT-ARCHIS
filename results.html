Design of Circuit

        Each of the two electrodes are connected between pins 2 & 3 of each of the 2 IC LF411. The output is serially transmitted from the pic microcontroller through the zigbee module and received on the computer.

Circuit Diagram

        The electrodes dipped in solutions mimicking the Intracellular Fluid and Extracellular Fluid solutions measured the potential difference between the two. These values were transmitted via the Zigbee module to a computer. Values between 50-60mV were displayed as normal (‘N’) and values deviating from this range were displayed as abnormal (‘A’).

Synthesis of SPIONS

  1. SEM Analysis:

            The synthesized SPIONs were seen under the scanning electron microscope. SPIONs with size ranging from 10-20 nm were observed as a cloudy dust. The Energy Dispersive Spectroscopy (EDS) peaks showed the exact iron percentage in Fe3O4.

  2. FTIR Analysis:

             The FTIR spectrum of the synthesized SPIONs showed bands at 574cm-1. This is the standard characteristic of magnetite.

Coatings of SPIONS with PEI

  1. SEM Analysis:

            The Energy Dispersive Spectroscopy of PEI coated SPIONS showed peaks for Carbon and Nitrogen along with that for Iron indicating the coating was successful.

  2. FTIR Analysis:

            The FTIR spectrum of the SPIONs coated with PEI showed a vibrational peak at 2922cm-1 and 1454cm-1 which represents C-H stretch and bend present in PEI.

Attachment of DNA

GEL Shifted Assay:

        The gel was run with the control lambda DNA in lane 2 and the sample of SPIONs bound to DNA in lane 3. The shift was examined under UV illuminator. It was observed that the control DNA travelled further than the sample seen as a faint band near the well.

MRI Result

        The PEI-coated-SPIONs are seen in the MRI as contrast, ghost-like images.

  1. T1W – T1 Weighted Image: A basic pulse sequence image that demonstrates the difference in T1 relaxation times of tissues.

  2. T2W – T2 Weighted Image: A basic pulse sequence image that demonstrates the difference in T2 relaxation times of tissues.

  3. PDW – Proton Density Weighted Image: The tissues with higher concentration of protons (hydrogen atoms) appear the brightest in this form of MRI scanning. Proton density weighted sequence produces contrast mainly by minimizing the impact of T1 and T2 differences with long TR (2000-5000ms) and short TE (10-20).

  4. BLISS IMAGE - BLISS is BiLateral Imaging in the Sagittal plane using SENSE. The BLISS sequence contains two sagittal stacks that can be planned and angulated separately. It is conducted for breast tissue.

Simulation

  • Formation of chitosan layer on PCB: Chitosan molecules tend to self-assemble onto a surface. Considering the thermal fluctuations and the energy released during the covalent bonding, it was observed that these molecules accumulate and form a uniform layer on the PCB.

  • Binding of SPIONs: The negatively charged origami linked to the SPIONs tend to compete to bind with the positively charged chitosan. However, repulsive forces are experienced by the SPIONs amongst one another due to a like negative charge. These repulsive forces combined with the limited surface area available for binding (due to comparable sizes of SPIONs and the chitosan-coated PCB).

  • Behaviour of nanobot in blood: The nanobot is seen to move like any other particle that is being propagated along a streamlined fluid flow. However, net forces acting on the nanobot varies due to the external magnetic field used to monitor the nanobot’s movement in blood. This is seen to give rise to an observable torque about the nanobot’s axis.

  • Behaviour of nanobot in interstitial fluid: a similar observation of the nanobot’s movements is made in the interstitial fluid, but the net forces acting on it is not as uniform as that experienced while travelling in blood. The most prominent force was hence considered - which is the net inward flux, arising due to the water potential, along with a minute factor of the resultant of random forces. The direction of these forces was randomised and the magnitude was fixed to facilitate easier conversions.

  • Piercing of cell membrane: the attractive forces between the phospholipid molecules consist of weak hydrophilic and hydrophobic interactions, maintaining its shape and rigidity. It was thus concluded that a piercing angle of 90 degrees to the plane of the membrane would be most efficient.
